1

The Research Process

How we approached brand development from first principles

01

Define the Business

We mapped the company's core purpose: placing reliable, unskilled and semi-skilled workers with tier-1 employers across 12 industry verticals. The brand had to speak to both employers (who need consistent labor) and workers (who need stability and respect).

02

Audit Competitors

We catalogued 10 major national staffing companies — their logos, primary colors, brand voice, and visual positioning. Every competitor was mapped on a color spectrum matrix to identify what was saturated and what was open.

03

Find the White Space

We identified the single color territory completely unoccupied by any major competitor in the labor staffing sector — and built the entire palette around it to create an unmistakable visual identity.

04

Build the System

We developed a 7-color, 3-zone system with every color pair validated for WCAG 2.1 accessibility compliance and hue-contrast clarity, then applied it across all touchpoints.

Blue Ocean Discovery

After auditing every major competitor, we found that teal is completely unoccupied territory in the labor staffing sector. Choosing Teal Deep as Steadfast's primary color means zero direct visual competition — your brand will stand alone on any job board, employer portal, or trade publication where staffing companies appear together.

Teal is also a Pantone 2026 trend color ("Transformative Teal") — positioning Steadfast as forward-thinking while every competitor looks dated in their 2010s navies and reds.

Brand Voice

Why This Brand Works

The strategic rationale behind every decision

Color Strategy

Why Teal?

  • Zero competitors use teal — instant differentiation on any surface
  • Teal communicates trust, stability, and forward momentum — ideal for staffing
  • Pantone's "Transformative Teal" is a 2026 trend — Steadfast looks current, not dated
  • Dark teal at L 12% provides excellent contrast for all text — accessibility built in
  • Teal pairs naturally with amber, creating a warm/cool balance no competitor has
Color Strategy

Why Amber?

  • Amber represents human warmth — the most missing quality in corporate staffing brands
  • As the widest (bottom) bar in the logo mark, it anchors the brand in people
  • Amber harmonizes with teal across the warm/cool temperature spectrum
  • Used only as fills and decorative elements, never as text — solving contrast failures
  • Associates with dependability and output delivered — exactly what staffing promises
Logo Design

Why Three Bars?

  • Three bars visualize workforce layers: oversight, semi-skilled, unskilled workers
  • Increasing width = growth — each level broader than the one above
  • The amber bottom bar symbolizes the essential human foundation of the business
  • Scalable to any size — readable on a business card, uniform, or billboard
  • Clean and geometric — projects professionalism alongside the largest staffing brands
Naming & Tagline

Why "Steadfast"?

  • The name itself signals reliability — the #1 quality staffing clients demand
  • "Workforce" is direct and category-clear — no ambiguity about what we do
  • "Steadfast Workforce" together = dependable people, every time
  • The tagline "Strength in Every Shift" reinforces work ethic and consistency
  • Name + tagline communicates the brand promise in under 5 words
