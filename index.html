1

The Research Process

How we approached brand development from first principles

01

Define the Business

We mapped the company's core purpose: placing reliable, unskilled and semi-skilled workers with tier-1 employers across 12 industry verticals. The brand had to speak to both employers (who need consistent labor) and workers (who need stability and respect).

02

Audit the Competitors

We catalogued 10 major national staffing companies — their logos, primary colors, brand voice, and visual positioning. Every competitor was mapped on a color spectrum matrix to identify which territory was saturated and which was open.

03

Find the White Space

We identified the single color territory completely unoccupied by any major competitor in the labor staffing sector — and built the entire palette around it to create an unmistakable visual identity.

04

Build the System

We developed a 7-color, 3-zone system with every color pair validated for WCAG 2.1 accessibility compliance and hue-contrast clarity, then applied it across letterhead, business cards, and digital touchpoints.

The Market Landscape

Who competes, what they look like, and where the gap is

🏭

$185B Market

US temporary staffing is a $185 billion industry growing at 4–6% annually, driven by labor flexibility demand from Amazon, Walmart, and manufacturing operators.

🎨

Navy & Dark Blue Saturation

7 of the top 10 competitors use navy, dark blue, or corporate blue as their primary color. The category is visually indistinguishable at a glance.

🧱

Red & Terracotta Second

Three companies use red or terracotta as a primary accent — communicating urgency but not warmth or reliability, the two qualities clients need most.

🔍

Zero Teal Presence

Not a single major staffing company uses teal as a primary brand color. This represents a complete "blue ocean" — uncontested visual territory in the category.

Blue Ocean Discovery

After auditing every major competitor, we found that teal is completely unoccupied territory in the labor staffing sector. Choosing Teal Deep as Steadfast's primary color means zero direct visual competition — your brand will stand alone and be instantly recognizable in any context where staffing companies appear together: job boards, employer portals, trade publications, and conferences.

This is not just differentiation — it is a strategic moat. Teal is also a Pantone 2026 trend color ("Transformative Teal"), positioning Steadfast as forward-thinking while every competitor looks dated in their 2010s navies and reds.

Why This Brand Works

The strategic rationale behind every decision

Color Strategy

Why Teal?

  • Zero competitors use teal — instant differentiation on any surface
  • Teal communicates trust, stability, and forward momentum — ideal for staffing
  • Pantone's "Transformative Teal" is a 2026 trend — Steadfast looks current, not dated
  • Dark teal at L 12% provides excellent contrast for all text — accessibility built in
  • Teal pairs naturally with amber, creating a warm/cool balance no competitor has
Color Strategy

Why Amber?

  • Amber represents human warmth — the most missing quality in corporate staffing brands
  • As the widest (bottom) bar in the logo mark, it anchors the brand in people
  • Amber harmonizes with teal across the warm/cool temperature spectrum
  • Used only as fills and decorative elements — never as text — solving contrast failures
  • Associates with dependability, harvest, output delivered — exactly what staffing promises
Logo Design

Why Three Bars?

  • Three bars visualize workforce layers: oversight, semi-skilled, unskilled workers
  • Increasing width = growth — each level broader than the one above
  • The amber bottom bar symbolizes the essential human foundation of the business
  • Scalable to any size — readable on a business card, uniform, or billboard
  • Clean, geometric — projects professionalism alongside the largest staffing brands
Naming & Tagline

Why "Steadfast"?

  • The name itself signals reliability — the #1 quality staffing clients demand
  • "Workforce" is direct and category-clear — no ambiguity about what we do
  • "Steadfast Workforce" together = dependable people, every time
  • The tagline "Strength in Every Shift" reinforces work ethic and consistency
  • Name + tagline communicates the brand promise in under 5 words
